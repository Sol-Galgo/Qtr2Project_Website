Tanks

AMX 13 105

It is a variant of the AMX 13 French light tank, with its modified FL 12 turret equipped with a 105-mm gun mounted on it. As production of the tank halted around the 20th century, we replicated the model as well as its individual parts using the blueprints that the top officials of the military that's based in France have given us (the specific details are not stated and are considered to be classified information).

Its main armament is equipped with an autoloader, it has really good top speed of 68km/h and an amazing engine power, which makes this tank able to easily traverse even the roughest terrain. It is also equipped with an amazing view range, and its low silhouette makes it difficult to get spotted by the enemy, especially if it's using camo. Although its armor is quite mediocre, even small-caliber guns can penetrate through it. This tank is best used for scouting missions and light infantry support due to its view range and small design.

Type 59

This tank was ought to be the Chinese-produced version of the Soviet T-54A tank. The turret is mounted with a 100 mm cannon as its main armament, with an additional 7.62 mm machine gun and a 12.7 mm anti-aircraft machine gun. Even though its production ended in 1985, approximately 10,000 individual tanks have been produced since the beginning of its production in 1958. Our company tries to restore the already existing Type 59 tanks and attempts to replicate and reproduce these tanks with the permission of the top officials of the Chinese military and the government (the specific details are not stated and are considered to be classified information).

The Type 59 has solid frontal armor with an angled hull that makes it capable enough to deflect certain shells and a rounded turret that's basically impervious to some tanks. It's also quite nimble, with a top speed of 60 km/h and an amazing hull traverse with an average of 46 degrees per second. Unfortunately, as this tank is based off of a Russian design, its maximum gun depression only sits at around -4°. In comparison, the usual gun depression of most British and American tanks sits at around -8° to -10°. Which makes it almost impossible to use in ridge lines and hilly terrain. This tank is best used for frontal engagements and urban warfare, as most terrains are usually flat in an urban setting.

Tortoise

It is an assault tank destroyer developed by Britain that was based on the design of the AT 15, which was also designed by Britain. The largest caliber gun that it can mount is the 120 mm AT L1A1 gun, which is the gun that we mounted on the tank. As only a total of five Tortoise tanks were developed during its production process, we were able to replicate the design and the parts of the tank using the blueprints given to us by the top officials of the British military (the specific details are not stated and are classified information).

The Tortoise boasts incredibly thick armor both on its front and on its sides, which increases the tank's survivability on the battlefield. Literally, almost every WW2 and even Cold War-era tank that faces this tank will have a difficult time penetrating it. Although it has really good armor, its downside is really awful mobility. Coupled with a horrible top speed of 20 km/h and awful engine power, it can definitely take quite a while to reach the battlefield. This tank is well suited for the frontline, more specifically frontal engagements against enemy tanks.

M60

The M60 was considered the primary main battle tank (MBT) of the United States during the Cold War. It ought to be the modernized version of its descendant, the M48 Patton. The turret is equipped with a 105-mm M68 gun. The tank first went into production around 1959, and around 15,000 copies of this tank were developed. Our company aims to restore these tanks and replace the broken equipment to our best extent, with collaboration with the US military, in order to ensure that these tanks will be effective on the battlefield.

This beast is equipped with amazing firepower, a sloped frontal hull, and a rounded turret that would provide maximum efficiency on the battlefield. It's also got pretty good mobility with 60 km/h top speed, making it able to zoom in on the battlefield. Not to mention, its huge cupola also gives the tank an amazing view range that would help the crew members spot enemy targets much easier. This tank's sole purpose is to be an all-round vehicle that can be used in most situations against other vehicles during its time, hence the term “main battle tank"

M109 Paladin

This vehicle isn't really considered a tank based on its definition, but a self-propelled artillery that's widely used in the United States. It has a 155 mm M126 howitzer and an additional .50 caliber M2 machine gun mounted on it. As it is still being produced to this day, we collaborate with various military companies that produce this vehicle to sell this product and to ensure its quality on the battlefield.

As this vehicle is a self-propelled artillery, this tank would not be well suited on the frontline of the battlefield when engaging against enemy vehicles. Instead, to maximize its efficiency on the battlefield, we can use this vehicle as support artillery. Situated at the very back of the battlefield, aiming at the enemy vehicles on the frontline.

Vertical Stabilizer

Does it ruin your accuracy when the tank moves so much? Are you always in a hurry that your last resort is to fire on the move? Well do not fret, as vertical stabilizer will solve all those problems for you. It lessens the dispersion of the tank while moving or turning the turret by stabilizing the gun, making it much easier to aim at your target.

Suitable Tanks: All Tanks
